I work on making machine learning models more efficient and reliable for real-world use. Data shapes modern ML systems — my goal is to design methods that let models interact with data more effectively so they are deployable under resource, privacy, and safety constraints.

My research centers on three interlinked directions:

  1. Data-efficient reasoning. How can models extrapolate from far less labeled data? Improving extrapolation and generalization under severe data constraints helps build robust systems for high-stakes settings.
  2. Test-time interaction with data. Can models be non-monolithic — efficiently consulting external sources (databases, the web, retrieval stores) at test time without prohibitive compute?
  3. Responsible data use. Efficiently auditing and controlling data and models — for privacy, fairness and safety — while preserving practical trade-offs with accuracy.

At Microsoft Research India, my work has focused on efficient grounding for retrieval-augmented models. For example, FrugalRAG explores how smaller language models can learn when to stop searching, cutting compute while preserving performance. I also co-developed LiveDRBench, a scalable benchmark for evaluating modern reasoning agents.

In collaboration with Prof. Vineeth N. Balasubramanian, I studied task transfer for vision–language models and identified clusters of related tasks that inform efficient fine-tuning strategies.

I’m particularly interested in methods that increase user trust and control — for example, our work on unlearnable text datasets (NAACL 2025) presents a framework that makes inadvertent model training on sensitive text data difficult without significantly changing its utility for humans.

I received my B.Tech (CSE) from Delhi Technological University in 2022. For more details see my CV or reach out by email.
